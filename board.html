Get In Touch

Dr Hazell Thompson Shokellu

Chairman

Dr Hazell is a Conservation Biologist with more than 25 years’ experience in NGO capacity development, teaching and research in ecology, ornithology and conservation. As a lecturer in Zoology at the University of Sierra Leone (His home country), he designed and introduced the first conservation biology course into the curriculum of any University in Sierra Leone.

He served as Regional Director of BirdLife International’s Africa Programme based in Nairobi for 10 years, leading and supporting a conservation programme spanning 23 counties. He also served as the immediate past Global Director, Partnership, Capacity and Communities, BirdLife International, responsible for capacity development and coordination in a unique partnership of 117 NGOs across the world.

His key motivation is the opportunity to make a significant difference not only to the environment, but also to people’s lives: “One of the greatest joys of my job is being able to help national programmes and organisations – and young people – to become stronger”. .

Dr Sam Ivande

Secretary

Dr. Sam Ivande is a Conservation Ecologist and Lecturer at A.P. Leventis Ornithological Research Institute (APLORI), University of Jos – West Africa’s foremost Ornithology and biodiversity conservation training Institute. Sam enjoys learning about the environment and since 2006 he has being doing this by studying and watching birds. Birds are colourful and highly mobile creatures and can be found in healthy habitats all around us.

They can move great distances in response to environmental changes and we can learn a lot about the state of our environment by observing them. One of Sam’s greatest desires is to encourage citizen participation in environmental conservation through bird watching. He is the pioneer and current Manager of the Nigeria Bird Atlas Project - a citizen science project encouraging public participation through bird watching for biodiversity conservation in Nigeria.

Dr Sonali Saha

Board Member

Dr Sonali Saha is a professor of Biology and Environmental Science at Miami Dade College, Florida, USA. Additionally, Dr Saha is a visiting lecturer of Ecosystems Ecology at APLORI, a University of Jo's affiliated institution.

Her research explores how plants interact with soil and groundwater availability and salinity with a focus on plant physiological mechanisms. She is an advocate of involving local communities in making decisions regarding utilization and conservation of natural resources management.

Prof. Ulf Ottosson

Board Member

Prof. Ulf Ottosson has a dedicated interest for nature, particularly birds that began as a young born which has given him a firm naturalistic base. He is a Freelance Ecologist based in Luxembourg. On completion of his PhD from Lund University, Sweden in 1997 he has worked as a freelance on different projects such as breeding biology in birds (Sweden), bird migration ecology (Canada, Nigeria, Russia and Senegal) and one more applied project.

He has worked as a part-time Senior Lecturer at APLORI since 2003. Apart from my scientific training at the University he has got a lot of experience in running and maintaining projects on expeditions, projects to the arctic and to the tropics on four different continents, mostly on different aspects of Avian Ecology. .

Adejo Rose Wisdom

Board Member

Adejo Rose Wisdom is the CEO and founder of the Better Earth Foundation. She holds a bachelor’s degree in Zoology and a Master’s degree in Conservation Biology from the A. P. Leventis Ornithological Research Institute, University of Jos, Nigeria. She is a conservationist biologist, an ecologist and an environmental advocate.

She is very passionate about the planet and nature conservation and hopes to promote environmental literacy among people as well as work to create awareness about the link between people and nature, particularly the younger generation. She believes that children are very influential and are the most potent force to reckon with, as they further influence their families and friends when they believe in a thing. Indeed, they are the agents of change for the environment. .
